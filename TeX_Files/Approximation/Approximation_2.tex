\bexo


What is the Lagrange interpolation polynomial  that at each point $x_j$ assumes the corresponding value $y_j$
with 
\begin{eqnarray*}
x_1=-1&y_1=3\\	
x_2=2&y_1=-3\\	
x_3=3&y_1=1\\	
\end{eqnarray*}

\begin{center}
	\begin{tikzpicture}
	  \begin{axis}
		\addplot [only marks,blue] coordinates { (-1.,3.) (2.,-3.) (3.,1.)};
				\addplot[blue] expression[domain=-1.:3.3,samples=500]{(3./2.)*x*x-(7./2.)*x-2};
		\end{axis}
	\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
 

\eexo
\solution{
We are looking for an interpolation polynomial associated to a set of $3$ points. Its degree is then $2$ and this polynomial is the sum of three elementary polynomials $P_i$. For each one of them the value of $P_i$ in $x_i$ is $y_i$ and equal to zero at the other points $x_j$ with $j\neq i$.
The expression of $P_1$ is:
\begin{align*}
P_1&=y_1\dfrac{(x-x_2)(x-x_3)}{(x_1-x_2)(x_1-x_3)}\\&=3\dfrac{(x-2)(x-3)}{(-1-2)(-1-3)}\\
&=\dfrac{1}{4}\left(x^2-5x+6\right)
\end{align*}
Similarily

\begin{align*}
P_2&=(-3)\dfrac{(x+1)(x-3)}{(3)(-1)}
=x^2-2x-3\\
P_3&=(1)\dfrac{(x+1)(x-2)}{(4)(1)}
=\dfrac{1}{4}\left(x^2-x-2\right)
\end{align*}
Hence, the interpolation polynomial is:
\begin{equation}
	P(x)=\dfrac{3}{2}x^2-\dfrac{7}{2}x-2
\end{equation}


}
