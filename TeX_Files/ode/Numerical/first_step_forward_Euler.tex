\bexo

Compute the solutions $\tb{S}_1$ and $\tb{S}_2$ of Forward Euler's method for the first two steps $\Deltat$ and $2\Deltat$ for a conservative mass-spring system with $x_0=0$ and $v_0=1$.
Show that this method is first order.
\eexo
\solution{
The first step is governed by the following two relations
\begin{equation}
	\tb{S}_1=\tb{S}_0+\Deltat\begin{bmatrix}
		0 & -\omega_0^2\\
		1 & 0
	\end{bmatrix} \tb{S}_0 \esp \tb{S}_0=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1\\
		0
	\end{Bmatrix}.
\end{equation}
Hence:
\begin{equation}
	\tb{S}_1=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}
\end{equation}
Similarily for the second step:
\begin{equation}
	\tb{S}_2=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-\omega_0^2\Deltat^2\\
		2\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}.
\end{equation}
The exact solution of the problem and its approximation for small $\Deltat$ at order $2$ are
\begin{equation}
		\tb{S}(\Deltat)=\begin{Bmatrix}
		\cos(\omega_0\Deltat)\\
		\dfrac{1}{\omega_0}\sin(\omega_0\Deltat)
	\end{Bmatrix}\esp \lim_{\Deltat \rightarrow 0}\tb{S}(\Deltat)=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-\dfrac{\omega_0^2\Deltat^2}{2}\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}+\tr{O}(\Deltat^3).
\end{equation} 
The first two orders are correct. The method is then first order. }
