\bexo

Compute the solutions $\tb{S}_1$ and $\tb{S}_2$ of Backward Euler's method for the first two steps $\Deltat$ and $2\Deltat$ for a conservative mass-spring system with $x_0=0$ and $v_0=1$.
Show that this method is first order.
\eexo
\solution{
One has
\begin{equation}
	\tb{I}_2-\Deltat[\bs{\alpha}]=
	\begin{bmatrix}
		1 & \omega_0^2\Deltat\\
		-\Deltat & 1
	\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
and 

\begin{equation}
	\left(\tb{I}_2-\Deltat[\bs{\alpha}]\right)^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{1+\omega_0^2\Deltat^2}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		1 & -\omega_0^2\Deltat\\
		\Deltat & 1
	\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}


One has then
\begin{equation}
	\tb{S}_1=\dfrac{1}{1+\omega_0^2\Deltat^2}\begin{Bmatrix}
		1\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-\omega_0^2\Deltat^2\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}+\tr{O}(\Deltat^3)
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
	\tb{S}_2=\dfrac{1}{(1+\omega_0^2\Deltat^2)^2}\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-\omega_0^2\Deltat^2\\
		2\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-3\omega_0^2\Deltat^2\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}+\tr{O}(\Deltat^3)
\end{equation}

The exact solution of the problem and its approximation for small $\Deltat$ at order $2$ are
\begin{equation}
		\tb{S}(\Deltat)=\begin{Bmatrix}
		\cos(\omega_0\Deltat)\\
		\dfrac{1}{\omega_0}\sin(\omega_0\Deltat)
	\end{Bmatrix}\esp \lim_{\Deltat \rightarrow 0}\tb{S}(\Deltat)=\begin{Bmatrix}
		1-\dfrac{\omega_0^2\Deltat^2}{2}\\
		\Deltat
	\end{Bmatrix}+\tr{O}(\Deltat^3).
\end{equation} 
The first two orders are correct. The method is then first order. }
